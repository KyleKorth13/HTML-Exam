    The first camera phonewas created by Phillipe Khan in 1997. There have been many versions of the camera phone built. The SCH-V200 was a cell phone with a built-in camera made by Samsung. It was able to take up to 20 photos at 350,000-pixel resolution. However, to get your photos you had to connect the phone to the computer. The phone and camera were basically two completely separate devices within a single body. The J-SH04 could take photos at 110,000-pixel resolution but unlike the SCH-V200 it was able to send photos electronically. The first US camera phone was the Sanyo SCP-5300. It was able to take pictures at 640x480 pixels. It also had many features like flash, zoom, a self-timer, and a few different filters. By 2003 over 80 million camera phones had been sold worldwide. The PM8920 could capture shots at 1280x960 pixels resolution. The pictures were good enough to print off and it had a feature where you could take eight quick pictures in a row. Samsung was the first to make a 5-megapixel camera phone but Nokiaâ€™s N95 was the first popular one. It had a Carl Zeiss lens and took great photos and could record a video at a rate of 30 frames per second. In 2008 Nokia released the first 8-megapixel camera phone that had a touchscreen that was called the LG Renoir. Camera phones kept improving their megapixel count as Sony made the first 16-megapixel camera phone. The improvement of camera phones was slowed down by the iPhone. It showed that there were other features than were more important than the camera. Also, the camera phones were big and chunky while iPhones were slim and attractive.
